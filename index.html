Understanding Heathrow Drop-Off Charges

If you're planning to drop someone off at Heathrow Airport in 2025, you'll need to be aware of the mandatory Heathrow Drop-Off Charge that applies to all vehicles entering the designated drop-off zones. Introduced in 2019 to reduce congestion and improve air quality around the airport, this fee affects all private vehicles, taxis, and ride-sharing services.

Heathrow Airport, located in West London, is the UK's busiest international airport and serves over 80 million passengers annually. The drop-off charge is part of Heathrow's broader environmental strategy to create a more sustainable transport system around the airport perimeter and encourage the use of public transport.

Key Facts:

  • Charge applies to all terminals (2, 3, 4, and 5)
  • Fee is per vehicle entry, not per passenger
  • ANPR cameras automatically track vehicles
  • Revenue funds sustainable transport initiatives
  • Part of Heathrow's commitment to net-zero carbon by 2050

How to Pay the Heathrow Drop-Off Charge

Paying the Heathrow drop-off charge is straightforward, with several convenient payment options available. You have until midnight the day after your drop-off to pay without incurring a penalty.

Online Payment

Visit Heathrow's official payment portal. You'll need your vehicle registration number and payment details. Available 24/7.

www.heathrow.com/dropoff-payment

Phone Payment

Call Heathrow's dedicated payment line: 0344 335 1801 (standard rates apply). Available 24/7.

Have your vehicle registration and payment card ready.

On-Site Payment

Payment terminals are located near all drop-off zones. Pay immediately after dropping off your passenger.

Accepts all major credit/debit cards and contactless payments.

Postal Payment

Send payment to: Heathrow Drop-Off Charge, PO Box 3351, Sheffield, S3 3WJ. Include your vehicle registration and payment details.

Not recommended - allow 5 working days for processing.

Payment Timeline:

  • Same day: Pay immediately after drop-off at terminals
  • Next day: Pay online or by phone before midnight
  • After midnight: Penalty Charge Notice issued (£80)

Exemptions and Discounts

While most vehicles must pay the Heathrow drop-off charge, there are important exemptions available:

Blue Badge Holders

Vehicles displaying a valid Blue Badge are completely exempt from the drop-off charge. The Blue Badge must be clearly visible in the vehicle's windscreen while in the drop-off zone.

Documentation Required:

  • Valid Blue Badge (not expired)
  • Badge displayed correctly (facing forward in windscreen)
  • Vehicle must be carrying the badge holder

Licensed Taxis

Black cabs and other licensed London taxis are exempt from the drop-off charge when operating in service. The taxi license must be clearly displayed.

Note: Private hire vehicles (Uber, Bolt, etc.) are NOT exempt and must pay the full £7.50 charge.

Public Transport Vehicles

Buses, coaches, and other forms of public transport are not subject to the drop-off charge. This includes National Express coaches and local bus services.

Airport shuttle buses between terminals are also exempt.

Important Note:

Exemptions are strictly enforced. If you claim an exemption without proper documentation, you may receive a Penalty Charge Notice. Heathrow uses ANPR cameras combined with manual verification for exemption validation.

If you want to avoid the Heathrow drop-off charge entirely, several free alternatives are available. While these options require a bit more planning, they can save you £7.50 per trip.

Drop off passengers at the free Long-Stay car parks and take the complimentary shuttle bus to the terminal.

Heathrow Express/Elizabeth Line Stations

Heathrow Central: 5-10 minute walk to Terminals 2 & 3

Perimeter Road Drop-Off Points

Use designated free drop-off points on the perimeter road where stopping is permitted without charge.

Location: Along the airport perimeter road, clearly signposted

Walking Time: 5-10 minutes to nearest terminal entrance

Note: Not suitable for passengers with heavy luggage or mobility issues

Penalties for Non-Payment

Heathrow Airport uses automatic number plate recognition (ANPR) cameras to monitor vehicles entering the drop-off zones. If you don't pay the required charge by midnight the following day:

£80

Penalty Charge Notice

Issued to registered vehicle keeper

£40

Reduced Penalty

If paid within 14 days

£120

Increased Penalty

If not paid within 28 days

Enforcement Process:

  1. ANPR cameras capture your vehicle registration upon entry to drop-off zone
  2. If no payment is received by midnight the following day, a PCN is issued
  3. PCN sent to the DVLA-registered keeper of the vehicle
  4. 14 days to pay reduced penalty (£40)
  5. 28 days total to pay before penalty increases to £120
  6. Failure to pay may result in County Court judgment and additional fees

Appeal Process:

You can appeal a PCN if you believe it was issued incorrectly. Valid grounds include: exemption eligibility, vehicle theft, or administrative errors. Appeals must be submitted in writing with supporting evidence.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I wait in my car while my passenger checks in?

A: No. The drop-off charge is specifically for the forecourt area where waiting is strictly prohibited. If you need to wait, use the Short-Stay car parks (which have different pricing starting from £5 for 30 minutes).

Q: Is the charge per person or per vehicle?

A: The Heathrow drop-off charge is per vehicle, regardless of how many passengers you're dropping off. One car = one charge, even with 5 passengers.

Q: Do I need to pay if I'm picking someone up?

A: No. The charge only applies to drop-offs. Pick-ups are free, though you should use designated pick-up areas which are separate from drop-off zones.

Q: How long do I have to pay after dropping off?

A: You have until midnight the day after your drop-off to pay the charge without incurring a penalty. For example, if you drop off on Monday at 3pm, you have until Tuesday at midnight to pay.

Q: What if I accidentally enter the drop-off zone?

A: If you enter the zone by mistake, you are still liable for the charge. However, you can contact Heathrow within 24 hours to explain the situation. They may waive the charge at their discretion for genuine errors.

Q: Are electric vehicles exempt from the charge?

A: No. Despite being zero-emission at the tailpipe, electric vehicles are not exempt from the drop-off charge. Only Blue Badge holders and licensed taxis qualify for exemptions.
